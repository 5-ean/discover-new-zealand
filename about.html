About

History


The history of New Zealand dates back approximately 700 years. It is one of the most recent settled regions in the world. First discovered and settled by Polynesian people, who over time developed the Maori Culture of kinship and connection with the land. The first European explorer known to sight New Zealand was Dutch navigator Abel Tasman on 13 December 1642. It was then later mapped by British explorer James Cook in 1769. This ultimately led to the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi in 1840, bringing New Zealand into the British Empire.

Wildlife


New Zealand is unique in that the island was mostly free of mammals before the arrival of humans. Because of this the island was dominated by other animal classes like insects, lizards (over 60 species) and flightless birds such as famous Kiwi, along with the Moa, with its species ranging in sizes from 12ft (3.6m) down to the size of a turkey. Sadly, it was hunted to extinction by native settlers. Today the ecology is being actively protected from the invasive species brought over by foreign settlers in the islands past, and conservation work has been ramping up in these modern times.

Activities


New Zealand contains some of the world’s most diverse and beautiful natural landscapes. Because of this, walking/hiking and camping are some the most popular activities. Milford sounds which is a combination of mountain peaks and lakes carved by glaciers during the ice age has been describe as the “8th wonder of the world” – Rudyard Kipling. The South Island hosted mountain resorts that in the winter attract Snowsports lovers from all over the globe. And in North Island during the summer/spring seasons rivers turn into cannoning and kayaking playgrounds.
